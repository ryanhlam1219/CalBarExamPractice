User Guide

Everything you need to know to practice California Bar Exam essays with AI analysis.

Quick Start

  1. Go to Practice and pick an essay question (or click Random Question)
  2. Write your essay in the editor — you have 1 hour on the timer
  3. Click Submit Essay — the AI scores your essay in two phases
  4. Review your results across six tabs of detailed feedback
Tip: Use headings in your essay (H1/H2/H3 buttons in the toolbar) to label each issue. The AI detects your headings and checks them against the expected issues from the grading template.

How the AI Grades Your Essay

When you submit an essay, the system assembles grading context from multiple sources and sends it to a local AI model in two phases.

Understanding Your Score

Your essay is scored across four dimensions that reflect the CalBar grading criteria:

Issue Spotting (0–35)

Did you identify the key legal issues in the question? Each missed issue costs points.

Rule Statements (0–25)

Did you state the applicable legal rules precisely, with correct elements?

Fact Application (0–30)

Did you apply the facts from the question to each element of the rule?

Organization (0–10)

Is your essay structured with clear IRAC format, headings, and logical flow?

Score Ranges

75–100Passing quality — strong analysis with most issues addressed 50–74Needs improvement — some issues missed or rules stated imprecisely 0–49Significant gaps — major issues missed, rules absent, or conclusory analysis

Essay Review Highlights

The AI marks passages in your essay with four types of highlights:

Strength Needs Improvement Missing Issue Structure
  • Strength (green) — Passages with strong rule statements or thorough fact application
  • Needs Improvement (orange) — Passages that need better rule precision or deeper analysis. Includes a suggested rewrite showing how a passing answer would state it
  • Missing Issue (red) — Issues you didn't address at all. The feedback explains what should have been discussed
  • Structure (purple) — Organizational feedback on IRAC structure, headings, and transitions

Data Sources

The AI grader uses three layers of legal context to evaluate your essay:

Schimmel Templates (Primary)

Prof. Sarah Schimmel's essay templates break each subject into issues, sub-issues, and required elements. This is the controlling structure for grading.

Supplemental Rules (Precision)

5,400+ rules from MyThemis outlines, BM25-ranked by relevance to your specific question. Provides precise rule statements and elements.

Selected Answers (Calibration)

Official passing CalBar essays for the same question. Key passages are extracted and used to calibrate scoring and suggest rewrites.

Essay Structure (Pre-processing)

Your essay's headings, IRAC patterns, and word count are analyzed before the AI sees it. Missed issues are flagged by comparing against selected answers.

You can see exactly what was sent to the AI on the Grading Context tab of any results page, including the visual flow diagram at the bottom.

Tips for Higher Scores

  1. Use headings — Label each issue clearly (e.g., "Holographic Will Validity"). The AI checks your headings against expected issues.
  2. State rules precisely — Don't just name the rule; state its elements. "A holographic will requires: (1) signed by testator, (2) testamentary intent, (3) material provisions in testator's handwriting."
  3. Apply facts to each element — For every element, explain how the question's facts satisfy or fail it. Don't be conclusory.
  4. Address counterarguments — Acknowledge the opposing side before concluding.
  5. Use IRAC consistently — Issue, Rule, Application, Conclusion for each issue.
  6. Check the Rule Funnel tab — It shows exactly which question facts the AI mapped to each legal element. Use this to understand what you missed.
  7. Read the selected answers — The Grading Context tab shows passages from official passing essays. Study how they handle the same issues.

Technical Details

  • AI Model: Ollama running locally (default: gemma4:31b-cloud). No data leaves your machine.
  • Database: PostgreSQL 16 stores questions, templates, rules, and analysis results.
  • Retrieval: BM25Okapi ranks supplemental rules and selected answer passages by relevance to each question.
  • Two-phase analysis: Phase 1 (scoring, temperature=0 for consistency) and Phase 2 (deep analysis, temperature=0.3 for nuance).
  • Privacy: Everything runs locally. Your essays are stored in your local PostgreSQL database only.
